# CC12.1 Antibody–RBD Interface: FoldX Alanine Scanning Report

**Complex:** CC12.1 antibody Fab (chains H, L) bound to SARS-CoV-2 RBD (chain A)

**Method:** FoldX 5.1 — RepairPDB → BuildModel → AnalyseComplex

**Residues scanned:** 12 RBD interface positions (chain A); position A475 excluded (already Ala)

**Date:** 2026-05-29

---

## 1. Overview

Alanine scanning mutagenesis was performed computationally on 12 RBD residues at the CC12.1 binding interface using FoldX 5.1. Each residue was individually replaced with alanine, and the change in binding free energy (ΔΔG_binding) was calculated using AnalyseComplex on the mutant and paired WT structures generated by BuildModel. Positive ΔΔG_binding indicates the wild-type residue contributes favourably to binding; negative indicates the WT residue is slightly unfavourable.

The WT baseline interaction energy for the CC12.1–RBD complex is **−17.87 kcal/mol**, consistent with a high-affinity antibody-antigen interaction.

## 3. Hot Spot Analysis

### N487 — Primary Hot Spot (ΔΔG = +2.57 kcal/mol)

N487 is the strongest binding hot spot at the CC12.1–RBD interface. The dominant contribution is **sidechain H-bond loss** (ΔΔG_sidechain_hbond = +3.15 kcal/mol), indicating that the asparagine side chain makes one or more direct hydrogen bonds with the CC12.1 antibody. This is partially offset by favourable solvation upon removal (ΔΔG_solvation_polar = −1.09 kcal/mol), which is typical — polar residues pay a solvation penalty for burial at an interface, so removing them partially relieves that cost.

N487 is part of the receptor-binding ridge (residues ~484–490) of the SARS-CoV-2 RBD, which is a critical contact region for many Class 1 and Class 2 neutralising antibodies.

### N501 — Second Strongest (ΔΔG = +1.84 kcal/mol)

N501 is a warm spot with an unusual energy profile: the dominant contribution comes from **backbone entropy** (ΔΔG_entropy_mainchain = +0.80 kcal/mol) rather than a sidechain contact. This suggests N501 contributes to binding partly through backbone positioning/rigidity at this end of the interface, in addition to a sidechain H-bond component (+0.69 kcal/mol). N501 is the site of the N501Y mutation found in multiple SARS-CoV-2 variants (Alpha, Beta, Omicron); this computational result is consistent with N501 being an important contact for antibody binding.

### K417 — Multi-term Warm Spot (ΔΔG = +1.56 kcal/mol)

K417 shows contributions from multiple interaction types: sidechain H-bond (+2.07), VdW (+1.27), and electrostatics (+0.74). These are substantially offset by a large favourable solvation polar term (−3.00 kcal/mol), which reflects the energetic cost of burying a charged lysine at the interface — removing it relieves that penalty. The net destabilisation (+1.56 kcal/mol) confirms K417 is a genuine binding contact.

This profile is consistent with K417 forming a **salt bridge** with a heavy-chain aspartate residue (D31 or equivalent), a well-characterised interaction for Class 1 RBD-targeting antibodies including CC12.1. The K417N/T mutations found in Beta and Omicron variants are known to reduce binding of Class 1 antibodies via disruption of this contact.

### L455 — Hydrophobic Warm Spot (ΔΔG = +1.30 kcal/mol)

L455 is driven by **VdW interactions** (+0.83 kcal/mol) and **hydrophobic burial** (ΔΔG_solvation_hydrophobic = +1.24 kcal/mol). This is a classic hydrophobic contact signature — the leucine side chain is packed against a non-polar surface on the antibody, and removing it to alanine decreases the buried non-polar surface area. No H-bond or electrostatic contribution is observed (+0.03 and +0.05 respectively), confirming this is a purely hydrophobic contact.

---

## 4. Anti-Hotspots

Three residues show negative ΔΔG_binding, meaning their alanine substitutions slightly *improve* binding.

**G502A (−0.93 kcal/mol)** and **T415A (−0.80 kcal/mol)** are the strongest anti-hotspots. For G502A, the improvement is driven by VdW (−0.44) and hydrophobic solvation (−0.75) — the alanine methyl group introduced in the Gly→Ala substitution makes a slightly favourable contact. For T415A, solvation polar improvement (−0.53) is the primary driver.

**Y495A (−0.59 kcal/mol)** is notable: the BuildModel analysis flagged Y495 as one of the top two hits (+6.20 kcal/mol), but AnalyseComplex shows it is actually an anti-hotspot for binding. Y495 destabilises the overall fold when mutated, likely due to packing effects of the large aromatic ring, but the residue itself is not a binding contact — its removal slightly relaxes steric strain at the interface.

These anti-hotspots are below 2σ noise for T415A and Y495A, so they are not strongly significant, but G502A (−0.93) is likely real.

---

## 5. BuildModel vs AnalyseComplex Comparison

BuildModel (fold stability ΔΔG) and AnalyseComplex (binding ΔΔG) are **different quantities** and should not be used interchangeably. The comparison reveals important discrepancies:

| Mutation | BuildModel ΔΔG | Binding ΔΔG | Verdict |
|----------|---------------|-------------|---------|
| G416A | +6.14 | +0.39 | False positive in BuildModel |
| Y495A | +6.20 | −0.59 | False positive in BuildModel |
| K417A | +0.48 | +1.56 | False negative in BuildModel |
| N501A | +0.40 | +1.84 | False negative in BuildModel |

G416A and Y495A score highly in BuildModel because mutating glycine (no sidechain) to alanine disrupts backbone packing and introduces steric clashes in the folded structure — but neither residue is making a specific sidechain binding contact. If BuildModel alone had been used, both would have been incorrectly identified as interface hot spots.

K417A and N501A are missed by BuildModel because their binding contributions involve specific H-bonds and entropy effects that are only captured when computing the interface energy directly via AnalyseComplex.

This comparison demonstrates why AnalyseComplex is the appropriate tool for identifying binding hot spots, and BuildModel alone should not be used for this purpose.

## 6. Energy Decomposition Notes

The five main mechanistic terms (sidechain H-bond, VdW, electrostatics, solvation polar, solvation hydrophobic) do not fully account for the total ΔΔG_binding in all cases. The residual "Other terms" (primarily VdW clashes, backbone H-bond, sidechain and mainchain entropy) can be substantial:

- **N501A**: Other terms account for +1.48 kcal/mol of the total +1.84 — entropy effects dominate
- **G416A**: Other terms = +2.09 kcal/mol (VdW clashes from the added methyl group)

All energy terms including decomposed ΔΔG values are available in `03_data/analysecomplex_master.csv`.

## 7. Limitations

1. **Single replicate per mutation.** FoldX best practice is 3–5 replicates to account for stochastic rotamer sampling. WT interaction energies varied by σ = 0.17 kcal/mol across the 12 paired runs. Mutations with |ΔΔG| < 0.34 kcal/mol (2σ) — S459A, G496A, Q498A — are indistinguishable from noise and should not be interpreted.

2. **Only the RBD side was scanned.** The CC12.1 antibody side (chains H and L) was not mutated. The antibody residues that contact N487, N501, K417, and L455 are unknown from this analysis alone.

3. **Glycine→Alanine mutations are not true alanine scans.** G416A, G476A, G496A, and G502A add a methyl group rather than removing a sidechain. Their ΔΔG values reflect backbone flexibility changes rather than sidechain contact loss, and cannot be interpreted the same way as other mutations in this set.

4. **A475 is a blind spot.** ALA475 is an interface residue (present in interface_residues.txt) that could not be scanned because it is already alanine in the wild-type sequence.

5. **FoldX force field limitations.** FoldX systematically underestimates the energetic contribution of charged residues and may not accurately capture all water-mediated contacts. Published FoldX alanine scanning has an RMSD of ~1 kcal/mol vs experimental data for binding ΔΔG.

---

## 8. Conclusions

Four RBD residues make significant contributions to CC12.1 binding:

- **N487** is the primary binding hot spot, contributing via a direct sidechain H-bond with the antibody
- **N501** and **K417** are warm spots; K417 likely forms a salt bridge with the antibody heavy chain
- **L455** is a hydrophobic warm spot with no polar contacts

G416 and Y495, which appeared as the top BuildModel hits, are structural artefacts and not binding hot spots. This underscores the importance of using AnalyseComplex over BuildModel for binding energy characterisation.

The identified hot spots — N487, N501, K417 — overlap with RBD positions mutated in SARS-CoV-2 variants of concern (N501Y in Alpha/Beta/Gamma/Omicron; K417N/T in Beta/Omicron), which is consistent with these variants reducing antibody neutralisation potency.
